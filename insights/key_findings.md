## Key Insights

### 1. Credit Grade vs Default Risk
- Default risk increases steadily from **Grade A to Grade G**
- Lower-grade borrowers account for a significantly higher number of defaults
- Credit grade is the strongest indicator of loan repayment risk

---

### 2. Loan Purpose Impact
- **Debt consolidation** loans contribute the largest share of defaults
- Small business and major purchase loans also show elevated default risk
- Loan purpose should be carefully evaluated during credit approval

---

### 3. Income-Based Risk Segmentation
- Lower and middle income groups show higher default concentration
- Higher income borrowers exhibit comparatively lower default risk
- Income segmentation helps identify high-risk borrower groups early

---

## Business Implications
These insights can help financial institutions:
- Improve loan approval strategies
- Adjust pricing based on borrower risk
- Focus risk monitoring on high-risk borrower segments
